# Climate Transition Risk Scenario Analysis Tool

## Overview

This project is an interactive data visualization platform designed to assess **climate transition risks and opportunities** across assets and portfolios.

It translates complex climate policy scenarios, emissions pathways, and economic assumptions into **clear, decision-ready insights**, enabling users to understand how the transition to a low-carbon economy may impact financial performance of an asset or a portfolio.

## What This Tool Does

- Evaluates **transition risks and opportunities** under different climate scenarios  
- Models emissions pathways and decarbonization trajectories  
- Estimates financial impacts from policy changes (level of carbon price)
- Includes a **stock analysis module** showcasing sample data from A-share listed companies’ transition risk dataset, designed for asset management companies with equity portfolios  
- Supports analysis aligned with **ESG, TCFD, and transition planning frameworks**

---

## Why This Matters

The transition to a low-carbon economy introduces complex and uncertain risks, particularly driven by carbon pricing policy changes.

---

## Methodology

### 1. Scenario-Based Transition Modeling

The tool applies internationally recognized NGFS' climate transition scenarios to simulate future economic and policy environments.

These scenarios reflect different assumptions about climate policy, technological development, and market behavior, enabling users to assess a range of possible transition outcomes.

---

### 2. Transition Scenario Framework

The model incorporates multiple transition pathways, including:

- Baseline (business-as-usual, assuming no additional climate policy action)  
- Current Policies  
- Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs)  
- Delayed Transitions
- Divergent Net Zero
- Net-Zero 2050 (i.e., 1.5°C scenarios)
- Below 2°C

Each scenario captures different levels of policy ambition, regulatory pressure, and market transformation.

---

### 3. Emissions and Risk Modeling

The tool models:

- **Greenhouse gas emissions trajectories** over time under each climate scenario
- **Emissions gap** between the baseline and target scenario
- Changes in **carbon pricing and regulatory costs**
- Estimated increase in transition costs, calculated by multiplying the emissions gap by the level of carbon prices  

These factors are used to evaluate how transition dynamics affect operational and financial performance.

---

### 4. Financial Impact Assessment

Transition risks are translated into financial metrics, including:

- Estimated **carbon cost** under different carbon pricing scenarios  
- **Carbon Value-at-Risk** (CVaR) to understand the impact on asset value
- **Implied Temperature Rise** (ITR) to understand the warming potential

The tool also supports the identification of **transition opportunities**, such as emissions reduction benefits and strategic positioning in low-carbon markets.

---

### 5. Data Integration

The model integrates multiple data sources, including:

- Climate scenario datasets (e.g., NGFS, CEADs)  
- Emissions data and sector benchmarks  
- Carbon pricing trajectories

These inputs are standardized to ensure consistency and comparability across scenarios and outputs.
